Intro

Get started

Note: This is demo content for a fictional SaaS company.

To talk about leadership, we must talk about a lot of things, one by one.

 

Direction

  • Leadership starts with a direction. First, we must know where we are going.
  • Before going anywhere, we must know why. We must have a purpose.
  • Then, we must know what we will find there. We need a vision.
  • But to actually get there, we need some sort of strategy, otherwise we will get lost.
  • Finally, we are ready to begin our journey.

Trust

  • We need to find companions whom we can fully trust and rely on.
  • Trust is the most fundamental building block of leadership.
  • To talk about trust, we must know what types of trust there are.
  • And we must also know that trust evolves over time, so we have stages of trust.
  • But trust is a complicated issue, and it depends on our states of mind.
  • It also depends on our feeling of safety, so we need to know the types of safety.
  • In order to create safety, we need to set different types of boundaries.
  • And trust can be easily broken if we are not careful due to our cognitive biases.

Communication

  • Trust is very much based on our interaction and communication.
  • Communication is a complex topic that includes multiple aspects.
  • We must understand the different styles of communication that people use.
  • We must also understand what types of feedback we can give and receive.
  • And also, what styles of appreciation and apology people prefer.

Coordination

  • Once we can manage trust and communication, we can start moving together.
  • To coordinate with each other, we must understand our leadership style.
  • And we must consider which followership styles align with our leadership.
  • We should also identify which sources of power are available to us.
  • And most likely, we will need help from one or more supportive roles.
  • Leadership is all about decisions, so we need a decision-making process.
  • We also need to acknowledge different decision-making styles.

Culture

  • When there is coordination and communication, there is a group culture.

Diversity

  • But to talk about culture, we must inevitably talk about diversity.

Maturity

  • If we want to grow, we must develop and increase our maturity.

Change

  • And our learning must lead to change, in both ourselves and our company.
