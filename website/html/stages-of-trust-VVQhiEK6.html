Stages of Trust

How much do we trust?

Note: This is demo content for a fictional SaaS company.

Stage 1: Baseline Trust

Baseline Trust = the initial level of trust we extend to strangers or individuals we barely know, often shaped by cultural norms, first impressions, and an optimistic outlook on potential relationships. This trust is typically superficial and influenced by politeness, rather than deeply rooted confidence in the other person's reliability or integrity.

How we encourage it:

  • We treat everyone with civility and professionalism to create a positive starting point.
  • We encourage polite and thoughtful communication as a standard practice.
  • We embrace diversity and encourage open-mindedness about different expressions of trust.
  • We establish safe spaces for discussions where employees feel secure to share.
  • We set clear expectations to create a reliable foundation for trust.
  • We encourage authentic kindness without pressuring for immediate reciprocity.
  • We balance openness with maintaining appropriate social boundaries.
  • We educate employees on trust dynamics to navigate first impressions effectively.
  • We celebrate and integrate diverse cultural norms to enhance mutual understanding.
  • We discourage assumptions that politeness equates to deeper trust, fostering genuine relationships over time.
Do

Manifestations:

  • We offer polite gestures, such as a smile or handshake, when meeting someone new.
  • We offer surface-level trust to encourage a positive start to relationships.
  • We rely on cultural values, like hospitality, to guide our initial interactions.
  • We assume positive intentions based on someoneâ€™s friendly demeanor.
  • We are influenced by first impressions, such as neatness or confidence.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • We equate politeness with genuine trustworthiness.
  • We believe that friendliness guarantees deeper honesty or integrity.
  • We assume everyone shares the same level of initial trust.
  • We think that trust can be immediately established without testing over time.
  • We expect others to offer the same baseline trust without hesitation.

 

Stage 2: Established Trust

Established Trust = the mutual understanding and commitment between individuals or entities to build a reliable and supportive relationship. It represents a conscious decision to create and nurture a bond that values consistency, honesty, and shared goals. It is a dynamic and ongoing process that requires effort, communication, and mutual accountability.

How we encourage it:

  • We prioritize transparency in all interactions to foster reliability and confidence.
  • We consistently meet our commitments and deliver on promises to establish credibility.
  • We embrace open communication channels to ensure clarity and understanding.
  • We build partnerships based on mutual respect and shared values.
  • We empower our team to act with integrity and accountability in all dealings.
  • We address conflicts constructively and seek resolutions that honor the relationship.
  • We celebrate and recognize contributions, reinforcing a culture of appreciation.
  • We maintain professional boundaries while fostering genuine connections to ensure trust remains authentic.
Do

Manifestations:

  • We show consideration by being polite and empathetic.
  • We actively seek to create positive and meaningful interactions.
  • We express appreciation and gratitude to reinforce the bond.
  • We respect each otherâ€™s boundaries and individuality.
  • We follow through on commitments, demonstrating reliability.
  • We share goals and collaborate to achieve mutual success.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • We exaggerate how well we understand each other.
  • We overlook clear communication, leading to misunderstandings.
  • We pretend to have greater trust than actually exists.
  • We rely on assumptions instead of seeking clarification.
  • We avoid addressing issues, fearing it will harm the trust.
  • We ignore signs of mistrust, hoping they will resolve on their own.

 

Stage 3: Developed Trust

Developed Trust = the process and state in which individuals or groups establish a deeper level of mutual reliance and confidence, characterized by the willingness to work together and accept differences while maintaining accountability and shared growth.

How we encourage it:

  • We lead by example through transparency in decision-making and actions.
  • We emphasize accountability at all levels to ensure commitments are met.
  • We actively celebrate shared successes to reinforce reliability and mutual respect.
  • We encourage feedback as a tool for growth, ensuring it is constructive and actionable.
  • We establish clear expectations to align efforts and reduce misunderstandings.
  • We offer training on empathy, conflict resolution, and effective communication.
  • We recognize and address breaches of trust swiftly and fairly to maintain credibility.
  • We share the rationale behind decisions to build understanding and support.
  • We regularly assess team dynamics and adjust approaches to maintain trust.
Do

Manifestations:

  • We openly communicate and express our thoughts and feelings.
  • We actively listen and strive to understand each otherâ€™s perspectives.
  • We hold each other accountable for our actions and behaviors.
  • We consistently demonstrate reliability through our actions.
  • We share experiences and build a foundation of mutual history.
  • We maintain transparency in our intentions, decisions, and actions.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • We assume that trust will naturally grow without effort.
  • We rely on others to do the majority of the trust-building work.
  • We hold on to unresolved conflicts, letting them undermine trust.
  • We expect perfection and become overly critical of flaws or mistakes.
  • We ignore the importance of aligning expectations and priorities.

 

Stage 4: Deepened Trust

Deepened Trust = the process and state of developing a profound and enduring confidence in one another, marked by a willingness to explore greater levels of emotional, psychological, and relational connection. It is a dynamic and evolving relationship quality that thrives on intentionality, growth, and shared vulnerability.

How we encourage it:

  • We foster a culture of open communication, where team members feel safe expressing their thoughts and concerns.
  • We offer support systems such as mentorship programs and peer collaboration to deepen relational connections.
  • We celebrate team achievements to reinforce mutual reliance and appreciation.
  • We train our leaders to navigate conflicts constructively and model trust-building behaviors.
  • We recognize and adapt to the evolving needs of employees, partners, and customers to maintain trust.
  • We acknowledge our limitations openly, demonstrating humility and a willingness to improve.
  • We protect boundaries, ensuring respect for privacy while encouraging authentic relationships.
  • We challenge the notion of trust as static, reinforcing it as a dynamic and ongoing commitment.
  • We engage in shared learning experiences to build empathy and strengthen bonds across all levels of the company.
Do

Manifestations:

  • We share mutual empathy and allow ourselves to be vulnerable together.
  • We effectively resolve disagreements, prioritizing understanding over winning.
  • We adapt our behaviors and expectations to meet each otherâ€™s evolving needs.
  • We communicate honestly, even when the truth is difficult to hear or share.
  • We support each other during challenges, providing reassurance and strength.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • We hesitate to share our vulnerabilities out of fear of rejection or judgment.
  • We avoid discussing conflicts, letting resentment quietly grow.
  • We rely too heavily on each other, creating an imbalance in the relationship.
  • We impose a sense of obligation rather than fostering genuine mutual care.
  • We view trust as a static achievement rather than a dynamic, ongoing process.

 

Stage 5: Sustained Trust

Sustained Trust = the ongoing, intentional process of maintaining and deepening mutual confidence, reliability, and faith in a relationship over time, requiring continuous effort, adaptability, and shared accountability. It evolves in response to lifeâ€™s challenges, changes, and growth opportunities, focusing on the health of the relationship itself rather than solely on individual actions.

How we encourage it:

  • We regularly solicit and act on feedback to maintain alignment and trust with those we serve.
  • We create safe spaces for open dialogue, encouraging honest discussions without fear of judgment.
  • We address mistakes with honesty and humility, focusing on learning and growth rather than blame.
  • We celebrate successes as a team, reinforcing mutual confidence and shared accomplishments.
  • We engage in regular reflection, evaluating our practices to adapt to evolving needs and challenges.
  • We provide ongoing development opportunities, building trust in our commitment to individual and collective growth.
  • We remain proactive in addressing potential issues, reducing the likelihood of trust being compromised.
  • We emphasize long-term relationships, valuing collaboration over competition within our teams and with our customers.
Do

Manifestations:

  • We view mistakes as opportunities to learn, improve, and deepen our trust.
  • We adapt to changing circumstances together, maintaining trust through lifeâ€™s transitions.
  • We reflect together on our relationship and ourselves, aligning on how to strengthen our bond.
  • We handle conflicts with a commitment to resolution, not avoidance or escalation.
  • We celebrate achievements and milestones as a team, reinforcing our connection.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • We take trust for granted and neglect the effort required to nurture it.
  • We fail to adapt to changes, clinging to old patterns that no longer work.
  • We prioritize personal agendas over the health of the relationship.
  • We let resentment build by not addressing hurt feelings or unmet expectations.
  • We assume trust will sustain itself without active care or effort.

 

Stage 6: Renewed Trust

Renewed Trust = the ongoing transformation of trust, brought about by navigating challenges, adapting to new circumstances, and fostering growth within a relationship or within oneself. This renewal reflects the ability to confront difficulties, overcome obstacles, and emerge with a stronger and more nuanced understanding of trust, making it more adaptable and enduring over time.

How we encourage it:

  • We foster a culture of open dialogue to address challenges and rebuild trust.
  • We train our teams to approach trust as a dynamic and evolving relationship.
  • We emphasize learning from setbacks as opportunities to strengthen trust.
  • We celebrate and acknowledge progress in restoring and renewing trust.
  • We revisit and refine our values to ensure alignment with trust-building practices.
  • We encourage forgiveness and letting go of past grievances to move forward together.
  • We highlight examples of successful trust renewal within the organization to inspire others.
  • We reward behaviors that contribute to the renewal and growth of trust among teams.
  • We support personalized approaches to renewing trust, recognizing each situationâ€™s uniqueness.
  • We partner with external advisors or mediators to assist in complex trust renewal situations.
Do

Manifestations:

  • We adapt our trust to align with new roles, stages, or circumstances in life.
  • We celebrate milestones that reflect the growth and transformation of trust.
  • We reflect on the ways trust has deepened through shared experiences.
  • We approach challenges as opportunities to strengthen our connection.
  • We build confidence in our ability to continually renew trust as life evolves.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • We assume trust will rebuild itself without active effort or communication.
  • We expect trust to return to its original state rather than embracing its evolution.
  • We neglect to reflect on how trust has changed or what it now requires.
  • We confuse the act of rebuilding trust with its ongoing renewal and growth.
  • We hold onto past grievances, undermining the potential for renewed trust.
