Communication Styles

How do we like to communicate?

Note: This is demo content for a fictional SaaS company.
PCM (Process Communication Model) = the psychological framework developed by Dr. Taibi Kahler in the late 1970s, focusing on the dynamics of effective communication by identifying and addressing different communication styles and preferences to enhance understanding and interaction.

 

 

Thinker Style (Analytical)

Thinker Style = the communication style characterized by logic, precision, and organization. It emphasizes clear, structured, and factual exchanges, focusing on providing and receiving information in a logical and methodical way.

How we support this style:

  • We prioritize data-driven decision making and clear metrics for success in all initiatives.
  • We structure our communication channels to maximize efficiency and clarity of information flow.
  • We encourage precise and methodical approaches to project management and execution.
  • We maintain clear documentation and structured processes for all major operations.
  • We utilize data visualization and clear reporting structures to communicate complex information.
  • We implement rigorous quality control measures in our communication protocols.
  • We maintain organized knowledge bases for efficient information sharing.
  • We value clear, concise communication that drives measurable results.
  • We implement structured feedback systems for continuous improvement.
Do

Healthy behaviors:

  • Delivering clear and concise information.
  • Organizing ideas in a logical sequence.
  • Asking thoughtful and relevant questions.
  • Staying focused on facts and data.
  • Breaking complex topics into manageable steps.
  • Using precise and accurate language.
  • Sticking to the main topic during discussions.
  • Prioritizing efficiency in communication.
  • Avoiding unnecessary tangents or distractions.
Don't

Stress-induced behaviors:

  • Overloading conversations with excessive details.
  • Focusing too much on minor issues.
  • Criticizing others for lack of precision.
  • Overanalyzing situations, leading to indecision.
  • Interrupting to correct small inaccuracies.
  • Using overly complex language unnecessarily.
  • Repeating points to ensure understanding.
  • Being overly formal or impersonal.
  • Losing patience with abstract or emotional input.

 

Persister Style (Principled)

Persister Style = the communication style focused on sharing values, principles, and purpose. It emphasizes conviction, dedication, and meaningful dialogue, with a preference for exchanges that align with beliefs and values while seeking recognition for commitment and loyalty.

How we support this style:

  • We create a culture where values and principles guide decision-making and strategic priorities.
  • We recognize and reward loyalty while maintaining focus on measurable results and outcomes.
  • We create clear frameworks for ethical business practices that drive sustainable growth.
  • We appreciate team members who demonstrate commitment to our profit-driven objectives.
  • We recognize dedication through performance-based rewards and advancement opportunities.
  • We value team members who show consistent commitment to excellence and results.
  • We maintain high ethical standards while aggressively pursuing market opportunities.
  • We appreciate team members who consistently deliver on their commitments.
Do

Healthy behaviors:

  • Expressing strong and clear convictions.
  • Acknowledging the contributions of others.
  • Asking purposeful and meaningful questions.
  • Focusing on shared principles and goals.
  • Demonstrating respect for differing opinions.
  • Providing constructive criticism with care.
  • Recognizing and appreciating loyalty in others.
  • Ensuring clarity of purpose in discussions.
  • Maintaining focus on the bigger picture.
Don't

Stress-induced behaviors:

  • Becoming overly critical of differing values.
  • Judging others harshly for perceived lack of dedication.
  • Lecturing instead of engaging in dialogue.
  • Overloading conversations with moral reasoning.
  • Becoming overly defensive when challenged.
  • Showing intolerance for ambiguity in decision-making.
  • Struggling to adapt to pragmatic solutions.
  • Dismissing input not aligned with personal priorities.

 

Promoter Style (Actionable)

Promoter Style = the communication style characterized by energy, enthusiasm, and persuasion. It emphasizes adaptability, charm, and quick thinking, often focusing on results and influencing others to take action through engaging and dynamic interactions.

How we support this style:

  • We reward those who can quickly close deals and influence stakeholders through charismatic leadership.
  • We create competitive environments where persuasive communication directly ties to compensation and advancement.
  • We maintain a fast-paced culture where quick thinking and adaptability are essential survival skills.
  • We structure our meetings to be action-oriented, focusing on solutions rather than dwelling on problems.
  • We value those who can rally teams around profit-driven objectives without getting bogged down in emotional discussions.
  • We implement training programs that enhance persuasive communication and negotiation skills.
  • We prioritize results-focused dialogue over consensus-building conversations.
  • We design our office spaces to facilitate impromptu interactions and quick decision-making discussions.
  • We recognize and promote individuals who consistently demonstrate the ability to influence and drive action.
  • We maintain an environment where enthusiasm and high energy are seen as indicators of commitment to success.
Do

Healthy behaviors:

  • Speaking directly and clearly.
  • Speaking with confidence and enthusiasm.
  • Adapting communication to fit the audience.
  • Inspiring action with persuasive language.
  • Maintaining a positive and energetic tone.
  • Keeping conversations focused on solutions.
  • Encouraging collaboration and teamwork.
  • Using humor and charm to build rapport.
  • Highlighting benefits and opportunities.
Don't

Stress-induced behaviors:

  • Dominating conversations with a forceful tone.
  • Becoming overly pushy or aggressive in persuasion.
  • Ignoring the input or opinions of others.
  • Interrupting or talking over others.
  • Using charm manipulatively to achieve objectives.
  • Becoming impatient with slower-paced interactions.
  • Over-promising and under-delivering.
  • Seeking constant attention or recognition.

 

Harmonizer Style (Relational)

Harmonizer Style = the communication style characterized by warmth, empathy, and a focus on emotional connections. It emphasizes building relationships, fostering harmony, and creating a supportive and inclusive environment where everyone feels valued and understood.

How we support this style:

  • We train our leaders to balance warmth with accountability in their communication style.
  • We create structured frameworks for providing supportive feedback that drives performance.
  • We value emotional awareness as a tool for building high-performing teams and increasing productivity.
  • We foster psychological safety while maintaining our competitive edge and drive for success.
  • We leverage emotional intelligence to negotiate better deals and create win-win situations.
  • We invest in relationship-building activities that have clear ROI for our bottom line.
  • We channel emotional awareness into better customer understanding and market positioning.
  • We support harmonious team dynamics that enhance productivity and efficiency.
  • We utilize emotional intelligence as a strategic tool for closing deals and driving growth.
Do

Healthy behaviors:

  • Expressing warmth and kindness in conversations.
  • Listening actively and with genuine empathy.
  • Offering supportive and encouraging feedback.
  • Validating the feelings and experiences of others.
  • Resolving conflicts with sensitivity and fairness.
  • Using inclusive language to foster connection.
  • Focusing on maintaining harmony in group dynamics.
  • Offering reassurance in stressful situations.
  • Showing gratitude and appreciation consistently.
Don't

Stress-induced behaviors:

  • Avoiding difficult conversations to prevent conflict.
  • Becoming overly focused on pleasing others.
  • Becoming overly emotional or reactive in discussions.
  • Resenting others for not reciprocating care.
  • Feeling overwhelmed by others' emotions.
  • Taking on excessive responsibility for group harmony.
  • Becoming passive-aggressive when needs are unmet.
  • Over-apologizing to avoid perceived conflict.
  • Becoming overly sentimental or nostalgic.

 

Imaginer Style (Reflective)

Imaginer Style = the communication style characterized by introspection, imagination, and a reflective approach. It emphasizes thoughtful and creative ideas, often preferring to step back, observe, and contribute through deep, independent reflection rather than direct interaction or immediate responses.

How we support this style:

  • We foster an environment that values deep reflection and encourages employees to share their creative insights at their own pace.
  • We provide flexible communication channels, including asynchronous methods like email or project management tools, to accommodate reflective thinkers.
  • We actively create spaces for introspection, such as quiet work areas or time blocks for uninterrupted thinking.
  • We prioritize substance in discussions, focusing on meaningful contributions over quick responses.
  • We encourage using visual or imaginative storytelling to communicate complex ideas effectively.
  • We provide training on effective articulation to help team members express their insights with clarity and confidence.
  • We design brainstorming sessions to include time for independent thought before group collaboration.
  • We encourage collaboration by pairing introspective thinkers with action-oriented teammates for balanced project execution.
Do

Healthy behaviors:

  • Offering thoughtful and reflective insights.
  • Taking time to carefully consider responses.
  • Observing and analyzing situations before engaging.
  • Using vivid imagery to explain concepts.
  • Demonstrating patience in conversations.
  • Maintaining a calm and composed demeanor.
  • Offering alternative viewpoints constructively.
  • Contributing quietly but meaningfully to discussions.
Don't

Stress-induced behaviors:

  • Withdrawing completely from conversations.
  • Becoming overly passive or unresponsive.
  • Losing focus on practical or immediate needs.
  • Ignoring pressing issues to focus on abstract ideas.
  • Struggling to articulate thoughts clearly.
  • Withholding valuable input out of hesitation.
  • Avoiding conflict by retreating into silence.
  • Struggling to adapt to fast-paced environments.

 

Rebel Style (Playful)

Rebel Style = the communication style characterized by playfulness, creativity, and spontaneity. It emphasizes engaging interactions, quick-witted responses, and an ability to think outside the box, often using humor and enthusiasm to build connections and inspire action.

How we support this style:

  • We value creativity and originality, integrating these traits into structured processes to drive innovative business outcomes.
  • We provide clear frameworks for brainstorming sessions, allowing spontaneous ideas to be refined into actionable strategies.
  • We highlight contributions that bring energy and enthusiasm to achieving measurable results.
  • We design collaborative spaces where creative thinkers can contribute meaningfully within a focused context.
  • We ensure humor is used appropriately to maintain professionalism while reducing stress in high-pressure situations.
  • We offer training to help team members balance playfulness with professionalism in client and stakeholder interactions.
  • We provide mentorship to help integrate creative and dynamic styles into structured, goal-oriented workflows.
  • We recognize and reward individuals who balance creativity with a results-driven mindset.
  • We protect time for strategic exploration, ensuring innovative ideas contribute to competitive advantages.
Do

Healthy behaviors:

  • Encouraging fun and enjoyment in conversations.
  • Using humor to diffuse tension.
  • Responding quickly and adaptively to changes.
  • Offering fresh perspectives in discussions.
  • Encouraging a relaxed and informal atmosphere.
  • Using quick wit to keep conversations engaging.
  • Bringing energy and excitement to group dynamics.
  • Balancing fun with productive contributions.
Don't

Stress-induced behaviors:

  • Becoming overly sarcastic or dismissive.
  • Using humor to avoid serious discussions.
  • Overusing humor, leading to misunderstandings.
  • Disregarding rules or norms to provoke reactions.
  • Struggling to focus on tasks or goals.
  • Ignoring others emotions or concerns.
  • Becoming defensive when faced with criticism.
  • Struggling to maintain consistency in communication.
  • Expressing frustration with exaggerated drama.
