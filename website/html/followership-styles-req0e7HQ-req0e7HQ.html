Followership Styles

How do we follow leaders?

Note: This is demo content for a fictional SaaS company.
Followership = the capacity and willingness to actively support, collaborate with, and follow a leader through behaviors, attitudes, and contributions that enhance the effectiveness and success of the group or organization.
Followership Styles = the various approaches individuals adopt in interacting with and supporting leadership, defined by their degree of independence, critical thinking, and engagement in organizational goals and activities.

 

 

Proactive Followership

Proactive Followership = the style of followership characterized by taking initiative, anticipating needs, offering solutions, and actively contributing to organizational goals. It reflects a high degree of independence, critical thinking, and collaboration, ensuring a productive and supportive relationship with leadership.

How we support this style:

  • We provide clear goals and expectations, enabling employees to anticipate needs effectively.
  • We celebrate proactive problem-solving and reward contributions that drive results.
  • We offer training programs to enhance critical thinking and decision-making skills.
  • We prioritize cross-functional collaboration to ensure alignment and proactive engagement.
  • We encourage employees to voice concerns and propose solutions rather than just identify problems.
  • We integrate initiative-taking as a core competency in performance evaluations.
  • We support mentorship opportunities to develop leadership traits among proactive followers.
  • We provide resources and tools that empower teams to address challenges head-on.
  • We invest in platforms and systems that make it easier for employees to act on ideas quickly.
Do

Benefits:

  • It builds trust and collaboration between leaders and followers.
  • It fosters accountability by modeling responsibility within the team.
  • It eases leaders' burdens by empowering followers to share duties.
  • It enables leaders to focus on strategy over operational details.
  • It boosts decision-making with diverse perspectives and feedback.
  • It strengthens leader-follower partnerships and team cohesion.
  • It fosters mutual respect as leaders value proactive contributions.
  • It strengthens leadership credibility by boosting team performance.
Don't

Drawbacks:

  • It can cause conflicts if followers excessively challenge leadership.
  • It may create confusion if leader-follower boundaries are blurred.
  • It risks overstepping authority, creating tension in hierarchies.
  • It could lead to a lack of cohesion if ideas clash within a team.
  • It risks power imbalances if proactive followers gain influence.
  • It requires leaders to be open to feedback, which may not occur.
  • It may cause leadership fatigue due to constant engagement.
  • It demands high emotional intelligence from leaders.

 

Pragmatic Followership

Pragmatic Followership = the style of followership characterized by a balanced approach where behavior adapts to the situation, balancing risks and benefits. It prioritizes practicality and self-preservation, avoiding extremes of passivity or over-involvement, and may appear neutral or ambiguous in loyalty and initiative.

How we support this style:

  • We encourage our teams to assess situations critically and act based on practical outcomes rather than rigid rules.
  • We provide clear guidelines but allow flexibility for employees to adapt to evolving circumstances.
  • We reward calculated decision-making that aligns with our business objectives, even if it involves creative deviations from norms.
  • We reward those who remain pragmatic, recognizing that not every situation calls for the same level of involvement or risk.
  • We empower employees to manage their involvement in projects based on their expertise and the situation’s demands.
  • We offer training that helps employees develop situational awareness and adaptability in dynamic environments.
  • We celebrate team members who maintain loyalty to the company’s mission while navigating complex scenarios.
  • We promote ongoing learning and skill development to help employees respond effectively to shifting conditions.
Do

Benefits:

  • It can adapt to different leadership styles and needs.
  • It fosters flexibility, enabling leaders to adjust strategies easily.
  • It supports practical decision-making, reducing impulsive actions.
  • It gives leaders a stabilizing presence in uncertain times or crises.
  • It ensures leaders can rely on others to stay focused on key goals.
  • It minimizes conflict by prioritizing practicality over confrontation.
  • It counterbalances overly idealistic or critical team members.
Don't

Drawbacks:

  • It can frustrate leaders seeking clear commitment or consistency.
  • It may cause ambiguity, leaving leaders unsure about loyalty.
  • It could foster a reactive team culture, slowing progress.
  • It risks damaging morale if followers seem detached or indifferent.
  • It breeds complacency, prioritizing "good enough" over excellence.
  • It encourages a “wait and see” attitude, delaying urgent actions.
  • It can reduce accountability if followers avoid owning outcomes.
  • It can undermine leaders' ability to inspire long-term visions.

 

Conformist Followership

Conformist Followership = the style of followership characterized by high engagement and commitment, with a strong strong tendency to follow authority and established norms, but limited independent critical thinking. It emphasizes obedience, loyalty, and completing tasks without questioning instructions or exploring alternatives.

How we support this style:

  • We provide clear and structured guidance to ensure employees understand expectations and objectives.
  • We establish strong leadership roles that offer consistent direction and decision-making.
  • We reward commitment and dedication to following established protocols and processes.
  • We create comprehensive training programs that instill confidence in following our systems and methods.
  • We recognize and celebrate those who execute tasks efficiently and faithfully without deviating from instructions.
  • We implement robust documentation and standard operating procedures for ease of compliance.
  • We build a culture of respect for hierarchy to ensure operational stability and smooth workflows.
  • We actively promote a sense of pride in upholding the company’s legacy and systems.
Do

Benefits:

  • It provides a stable environment for leaders to execute decisions.
  • It reduces resistance, allowing leaders to focus on strategy.
  • It builds trust in leadership by reinforcing loyalty and commitment.
  • It simplifies communication, with prompt action on instructions.
  • It provides leaders with dependable, task-oriented support.
  • It ensures adherence to organizational standards and policies.
  • It aids quick implementation of leadership decisions during crises.
Don't

Drawbacks:

  • It limits the diversity of ideas and perspectives in problem-solving.
  • It promotes groupthink and limits the ability to identify risks.
  • It creates a passive organizational culture resistant to change.
  • It shifts responsibility to leaders, reducing personal accountability.
  • It reduces opportunities for growth and development in followers.
  • It risks perpetuating outdated or ineffective practices.
  • It might lead to repeated errors due to lack of critical review.

 

Passive Followership

Passive Followership = the style of followership characterized by a lack of initiative, autonomy, and critical thinking, where individuals rely heavily on leaders for direction, avoid responsibility, and contribute little to decision-making, problem-solving, or active engagement.

How we support this style:

  • We provide clear instructions and structured workflows to minimize ambiguity for passive followers.
  • We establish well-defined roles and responsibilities, ensuring expectations are straightforward and easy to follow.
  • We leverage systems and tools that guide daily tasks and reduce the need for independent decision-making.
  • We implement frequent check-ins to ensure alignment and provide reassurance to those who prefer direction.
  • We encourage collaboration in controlled environments to help passive followers contribute without feeling overwhelmed.
  • We reward compliance and execution of instructions as a form of contribution to company goals.
  • We pair passive followers with strong mentors or supervisors to provide ongoing support and direction.
  • We cultivate an environment where taking initiative is encouraged but not required for success.
Do

Benefits:

  • It promotes consistency by centralizing control under a leader.
  • It eases coordination as followers align with the leader's directives.
  • It fosters efficiency by eliminating unnecessary debates or delays.
  • It creates a structured environment for inexperienced teams.
  • It enables leaders to take full accountability for team outcomes.
  • It allows leaders to implement their vision without resistance.
Don't

Drawbacks:

  • It creates overdependence on leaders, weakening team resilience.
  • It fosters a lack of engagement and motivation within the team.
  • It increases the leader’s workload by discouraging delegation.
  • It causes inefficiency if leaders make poor decisions alone.
  • It creates a bottleneck when leaders are absent or overwhelmed.
  • It misses out on team members' diverse talents and perspectives.
  • It limits personal growth and development of followers.

 

Alienated Followership

Alienated Followership = the style of followership characterized by independent and critical thinking combined with low engagement and commitment toward leadership or organizational goals. It questions authority without actively contributing, often stemming from dissatisfaction, mistrust, or perceived unfairness.

How we support this style:

  • We provide platforms for voicing dissent in constructive ways.
  • We create opportunities for these individuals to channel their critiques into actionable solutions.
  • We establish transparent processes to rebuild trust where it may have been lost.
  • We invite alienated followers to participate in strategic decision-making forums.
  • We develop training programs to transform independent thought into collaborative action.
  • We address dissatisfaction promptly to minimize feelings of alienation.
  • We assign roles where their critical thinking adds value without relying heavily on their engagement.
  • We invest in mentorship to help alienated followers align their perspectives with organizational goals.
  • We avoid punitive responses to dissent, fostering a culture of respect and inclusion.
Do

Benefits:

  • It enhances accountability by requiring leaders to justify decisions.
  • It reveals weaknesses in leadership and organizational goals.
  • It highlights areas where leadership communication can improve.
  • It offers alternative perspectives that may lead to better solutions.
  • It uncovers overlooked issues within the team or organization.
  • It drives leaders to address dissatisfaction and rebuild trust.
  • It stimulates growth by challenging the status quo.
Don't

Drawbacks:

  • It undermines leadership authority, leading to decreased morale.
  • It damages trust between leaders and team members.
  • It can delay decision-making through excessive questioning.
  • It fosters disengagement, reducing team cohesion.
  • It creates a negative culture through constant dissatisfaction.
  • It discourages leaders from taking risks for fear of backlash.
  • It leads to burnout in leadership by requiring constant justification.
  • It creates resistance to change or new initiatives.
