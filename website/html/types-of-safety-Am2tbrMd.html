Types of Safety

Do we feel safe?

Note: This is demo content for a fictional SaaS company.

Physical Safety

Physical Safety = the condition of being protected from physical harm or injury, ensuring that an individual's body is secure from accidents, violence, or environmental hazards.

How we create safety:

  • We ensure all workplaces comply with safety regulations and standards.
  • We conduct regular safety audits to identify and mitigate risks.
  • We provide ergonomic workspaces to minimize physical strain.
  • We invest in high-quality safety equipment for relevant roles.
  • We maintain clear evacuation procedures for all locations.
  • We offer regular training on workplace safety and emergency response.
  • We encourage reporting of hazards without fear of retaliation.
  • We partner with experts to improve safety measures continuously.
  • We prioritize mental and physical well-being in our safety policies.
  • We celebrate a culture of safety by recognizing and rewarding compliance.
Do

When safety is present:

  • Workers focus on creativity rather than fear of hazards.
  • Management is seen as responsible and caring for employee well-being.
  • Fewer accidents mean less downtime and lower healthcare costs.
  • A safe and stable workforce projects reliability to customers.
Don't

When safety is missing:

  • Accidents result in lost workdays and higher medical expenses.
  • Fear of harm leads to stress, dissatisfaction, and disengagement.
  • Workplace accidents can result in lawsuits and hefty fines.
  • Fear and incidents can lead to mistrust between employees and management.

 

Digital Safety

Digital Safety = the condition of being protected from risks and threats in the digital environment, ensuring the security of personal information, devices, and online activities from unauthorized access, cyberattacks, and malicious activities.

How we create safety:

  • We implement strong encryption protocols to protect sensitive data.
  • We regularly update systems and software to address security vulnerabilities.
  • We enforce multi-factor authentication for all digital accounts.
  • We conduct frequent cybersecurity training for all team members.
  • We partner with experts to monitor and strengthen our defenses.
  • We maintain clear incident response plans for potential breaches.
  • We restrict access to sensitive information based on roles and responsibilities.
  • We regularly test our systems through penetration testing and audits.
  • We encourage employees to report suspicious activity without hesitation.
  • We stay informed on emerging threats and adapt our strategies accordingly.
Do

When safety is present:

  • Employees confidently use digital tools without fear of data loss or breaches.
  • Regular training enhances staff awareness of cybersecurity best practices.
  • Secure systems enable remote work, giving employees flexibility and confidence.
  • Employees easily report suspicious activity, fostering a proactive culture.
Don't

When safety is missing:

  • Workers are reluctant to use digital tools, fearing exposure to cyber risks.
  • Inadequate cybersecurity causes employees to distrust workplace systems.
  • Breaches lead to disciplinary actions against employees, even for mistakes.
  • Stress from handling frequent breaches contributes to employee burnout.

 

Emotional Safety

Emotional Safety = the condition of feeling secure and supported in expressing emotions and vulnerabilities without fear of judgment, rejection, or harm, fostering trust, empathy, and mutual respect in relationships and environments.

How we create safety:

  • We foster a culture of respect, empathy, and inclusivity.
  • We promote open communication and active listening at all levels.
  • We provide access to mental health resources and support programs.
  • We address conflicts promptly and fairly, prioritizing resolution over blame.
  • We train leaders to create psychologically safe environments for their teams.
  • We encourage feedback without fear of retaliation or dismissal.
  • We celebrate diversity and ensure all voices are valued and heard.
  • We discourage toxic behavior and hold individuals accountable for their actions.
  • We offer flexible policies that support work-life balance and reduce stress.
  • We continuously assess our workplace culture to identify and address concerns.
Do

When safety is present:

  • Employees feel comfortable expressing their opinions without fear of judgment.
  • Workers feel valued and respected, boosting morale and engagement.
  • Team dynamics improve as employees trust and rely on one another.
  • Employees trust that leadership will act on concerns and feedback.
Don't

When safety is missing:

  • Toxic behavior, such as bullying or harassment, goes unaddressed.
  • Fear of retribution prevents employees from reporting issues or mistakes.
  • Poor communication fosters misunderstandings and workplace tension.
  • Employees avoid taking risks, stifling creativity and innovation.

 

Psychological Safety

Psychological Safety = the condition of feeling secure and confident in contributing ideas, expressing concerns, and taking risks without fear of embarrassment, ridicule, or retribution, promoting open communication and collaboration in a supportive environment.

How we create safety:

  • We encourage team members to speak up without fear of judgment or reprisal.
  • We promote a blame-free culture where mistakes are seen as opportunities to learn.
  • We ensure that feedback, both given and received, is constructive and respectful.
  • We empower employees to take calculated risks and share innovative ideas.
  • We train leaders to create supportive environments that value vulnerability and openness.
  • We prioritize clarity in expectations and decision-making to reduce uncertainty.
  • We recognize and celebrate contributions, regardless of hierarchy or role.
  • We provide platforms for anonymous feedback to uncover hidden concerns.
  • We address biases and foster equity in all processes and interactions.
  • We regularly check in with teams to assess their comfort and confidence levels.
Do

When safety is present:

  • Teams embrace diverse perspectives, fostering creativity and collaboration.
  • Employees are comfortable acknowledging mistakes, enabling continuous learning.
  • Constructive feedback is delivered in ways that encourage growth, not defensiveness.
  • Workers trust their colleagues to act with integrity and professionalism.
Don't

When safety is missing:

  • Employees fear sharing ideas, worrying about criticism or dismissal.
  • Team members hesitate to ask questions, hindering learning and growth.
  • Toxic behavior, such as public shaming, discourages participation in discussions.
  • Conflict resolution is avoided, allowing tensions to escalate and fester.

 

Moral Safety

Moral Safety = the condition of being protected from situations or influences that conflict with ones ethical principles or values, ensuring an environment that respects and upholds individual and collective moral integrity.

How we create safety:

  • We uphold a clear code of ethics that guides all decisions and behaviors.
  • We foster a culture of integrity, where doing the right thing is always prioritized.
  • We ensure transparency in our actions and communication.
  • We encourage employees to voice concerns about unethical practices without fear.
  • We provide training to help teams recognize and navigate ethical dilemmas.
  • We hold everyone, including leadership, accountable to the same moral standards.
  • We maintain fair and equitable treatment in all policies and interactions.
  • We prioritize sustainability and social responsibility in our operations.
  • We build trust by consistently aligning our actions with our stated values.
  • We regularly review and adapt our practices to reflect evolving ethical standards.
Do

When safety is present:

  • Employees feel assured that their ethical concerns will be taken seriously and addressed fairly.
  • Workers trust that reporting unethical behavior will not lead to retaliation.
  • Employees feel aligned with the companys values, boosting job satisfaction.
  • Strong moral safety fosters long-term loyalty and trust among employees.
Don't

When safety is missing:

  • Employees fear retaliation for reporting unethical behavior, leading to silence.
  • Workers feel pressure to compromise their values to meet workplace demands.
  • Unethical behavior by colleagues creates a toxic and demoralizing environment.
  • Favoritism and double standards undermine trust in fairness and integrity.

 

Social Safety

Social Safety = the condition of feeling secure and accepted within a community or group, free from social exclusion, discrimination, or harm, fostering a sense of belonging, trust, and mutual respect.

How we create safety:

  • We cultivate a culture of inclusivity where everyone feels they belong.
  • We celebrate diverse perspectives and encourage respectful dialogue.
  • We establish zero tolerance for bullying, harassment, or discrimination.
  • We promote teamwork and collaboration to strengthen interpersonal connections.
  • We provide clear channels for reporting social concerns without fear of reprisal.
  • We host events and initiatives to build community and camaraderie among employees.
  • We train leaders to recognize and address social dynamics that may hinder safety.
  • We ensure that everyone has equal access to opportunities and resources.
  • We regularly seek feedback to understand and improve social experiences at work.
  • We lead by example, demonstrating kindness, respect, and empathy in all interactions.
Do

When safety is present:

  • Employees feel accepted and included as valued members of their teams.
  • Open dialogue about cultural differences fosters mutual respect and understanding.
  • Team members support each other during challenging times, building strong relationships.
  • Employees actively participate in workplace initiatives, feeling part of a community.
Don't

When safety is missing:

  • Employees avoid social interactions, leading to a lack of collaboration.
  • Discrimination or microaggressions go unchecked, alienating employees.
  • Workers feel unwelcome or unsupported during team projects.
  • New employees struggle to integrate due to a lack of social support systems.

 

Financial Safety

Financial Safety = the condition of having stable and secure financial resources, ensuring the ability to meet basic needs, manage expenses, and withstand economic uncertainties without undue stress or risk of financial harm.

How we create safety:

  • We provide competitive and fair compensation aligned with market standards.
  • We offer transparent pay structures to build trust and fairness.
  • We ensure timely and accurate salary payments without delays.
  • We provide financial literacy resources to help employees manage their finances.
  • We offer robust benefits, including retirement plans and savings options.
  • We maintain emergency funds to protect the company and employees during downturns.
  • We prioritize sustainable growth to ensure long-term job security.
  • We offer clear paths for career advancement tied to financial growth opportunities.
  • We protect employees from unethical financial practices or undue pressure.
  • We communicate openly about company financial health and any potential impacts on employees.
Do

When safety is present:

  • Employees can plan their future confidently, knowing their income is stable and predictable.
  • Financial stability at work reduces overall stress and allows employees to focus on their tasks.
  • Workers are less likely to seek other jobs when they feel financially safe in their current role.
  • Employees perceive the company as a stable and dependable employer, boosting organizational reputation.
Don't

When safety is missing:

  • Employees worry about their ability to pay for essentials like rent, food, and utilities.
  • Financial instability prevents employees from committing to long-term financial plans.
  • Fears of potential job loss due to financial instability within the company undermine morale.
  • Employees seek side jobs or additional work to compensate for the lack of financial security.

 

Professional Safety

Professional Safety = the condition of being secure in ones workplace or career, free from discrimination, harassment, or undue risk, while having the support and resources needed to perform effectively, grow, and maintain job stability.

How we create safety:

  • We provide clear job descriptions and expectations to reduce ambiguity.
  • We ensure fair and transparent performance evaluations.
  • We support professional growth with training, mentorship, and development programs.
  • We protect employees from unfair treatment, favoritism, or retaliation.
  • We encourage constructive feedback to help employees improve without fear of judgment.
  • We foster a culture of recognition, where contributions are acknowledged and valued.
  • We offer clear grievance mechanisms for resolving professional disputes.
  • We empower employees to take ownership of their roles while providing support.
  • We ensure that promotions and opportunities are based on merit and fairness.
  • We protect employees reputations by addressing concerns privately and respectfully.
Do

When safety is present:

  • Employees feel confident about their career growth prospects within the organization.
  • Workers trust that performance evaluations are fair and based on merit.
  • Workers feel supported in their career aspirations, leading to increased job satisfaction.
  • Employees have confidence that decisions about promotions and assignments are equitable.
Don't

When safety is missing:

  • Employees fear sudden layoffs or job losses, creating a pervasive sense of insecurity.
  • Employees feel undervalued when their contributions are not recognized or rewarded.
  • Workers avoid voicing concerns or ideas, fearing it could harm their reputation or job stability.
  • High turnover rates create an unstable environment, increasing employee stress.
