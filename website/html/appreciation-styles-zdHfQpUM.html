Appreciation Styles

How do we show appreciation?

Note: This is demo content for a fictional SaaS company.
Appreciation = the act of recognizing and valuing the worth, quality, or significance of someone or something, often expressed through gratitude, admiration, or respect.
Languages of Appreciation = the various ways individuals prefer to give and receive recognition and gratitude, emphasizing personalized communication to build stronger relationships. Introduced by Dr. Gary Chapman and Dr. Paul White in 2011, this framework is designed to enhance workplace culture and interpersonal understanding.

 

 

Words of Affirmation

Words of Affirmation = the expression of appreciation, encouragement, or praise through spoken or written words, used to convey value, recognition, or gratitude to others. It can include compliments, acknowledgments, or encouraging statements that affirm the recipient's efforts or qualities.

How we support this style:

  • We recognize and celebrate the achievements of our team members through regular praise during meetings and in written communications.
  • We make a habit of acknowledging individual and team milestones with personalized messages of appreciation.
  • We ensure that managers are trained to give constructive and affirming feedback frequently.
  • We publicly recognize exceptional performance to inspire and motivate others.
  • We highlight employee contributions in company-wide newsletters or announcements.
  • We provide leaders with tools and templates to help them articulate meaningful appreciation effectively.
  • We empower managers to celebrate small wins with words of encouragement to maintain morale and momentum.
  • We use company events as a platform to share stories of individual and team successes.
Do

Manifestations:

  • Praising someone for their effort on a recent task.
  • Writing a personalized thank-you email for a contribution.
  • Highlighting someone’s achievement in a team discussion.
  • Acknowledging an individual’s unique skills during a meeting.
  • Sending a thoughtful note recognizing hard work.
  • Sharing appreciation for someone’s supportive attitude.
  • Verbalizing gratitude during a team celebration or milestone event.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Giving vague praise that lacks detail or sincerity.
  • Using repetitive phrases that feel robotic or scripted.
  • Using flattery that feels superficial or excessive.
  • Praising only results without acknowledging effort.
  • Failing to acknowledge key contributions in a group setting.
  • Combining praise with criticism, undermining the positive impact.
  • Praising one individual repeatedly while ignoring others.
  • Failing to notice or recognize quieter contributors.

 

Acts of Service

Acts of Service = the expression of appreciation or care through practical actions that help, support, or ease the burden of others. It involves taking initiative to assist with tasks or responsibilities, demonstrating value and consideration through tangible efforts.

How we support this style:

  • We support professional development by covering logistical burdens, such as arranging training or certifications.
  • We encourage collaboration by fostering a culture where teammates actively support each other on projects.
  • We proactively address pain points in workflows and remove obstacles to ensure smoother operations.
  • We take initiative to offer assistance when employees face personal challenges, showing care through actionable support.
  • We empower managers to step in and lead by example, taking on tasks when teams are stretched thin.
  • We celebrate accomplishments by handling the details of recognition events, allowing employees to simply enjoy the moment.
  • We maintain a dedicated team to support employee initiatives, from innovation projects to community service events.
  • We regularly check in with employees to identify specific needs and provide tailored assistance where it’s most impactful.
Do

Manifestations:

  • Helping a colleague meet a tight deadline by sharing workload.
  • Handling routine tasks to free a team member for priority work.
  • Volunteering to cover for someone who is unwell or on leave.
  • Volunteering to take on extra responsibilities during busy periods.
  • Assisting with setting up or cleaning after an event.
  • Preparing coffee or snacks for a busy team.
  • Taking meeting notes and sharing them with the team.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Helping without asking, which may disrupt someone’s workflow.
  • Assuming someone needs help instead of communicating first.
  • Offering help but not following through on the commitment.
  • Offering services that are unwanted or unhelpful.
  • Expecting acknowledgment or repayment for every act.
  • Taking on tasks reluctantly or with visible frustration.
  • Providing inconsistent or half-hearted support.

 

Quality Time

Quality Time = the expression of appreciation or care by giving undivided attention and being fully present in shared moments, fostering connection and mutual understanding through meaningful interactions.

How we support this style:

  • We hold regular one-on-one meetings where leaders give employees their undivided attention to discuss goals, challenges, and growth opportunities.
  • We host team retreats or offsite events focused on meaningful collaboration and connection beyond daily work tasks.
  • We create dedicated time in meetings for open dialogue, ensuring everyone’s voice is heard and valued.
  • We organize workshops and brainstorming sessions where employees can engage deeply with peers and leadership.
  • We encourage team lunches or coffee breaks to build camaraderie and foster quality conversations.
  • We offer flexible time for mentorship sessions, allowing employees and mentors to connect deeply and without interruptions.
  • We host town halls or all-hands meetings where leadership dedicates time to answering employee questions and engaging in dialogue.
  • We actively create moments for teams to bond through shared activities like volunteer days or collaborative projects.
Do

Manifestations:

  • Scheduling one-on-one meetings to connect with a colleague.
  • Actively listening during conversations without distractions.
  • Spending time brainstorming or problem-solving together.
  • Setting aside time to mentor or coach someone.
  • Attending important events or presentations to show support.
  • Spending time to provide constructive feedback in person.
  • Showing up early or staying late to work alongside someone.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Being distracted by phones or emails during interactions.
  • Rushing through or interrupting meetings and conversations.
  • Cancelling or rescheduling time together without valid reasons.
  • Focusing on tasks rather than the person during shared time.
  • Making shared time feel transactional or obligatory.
  • Failing to show genuine interest in the other person’s perspective.
  • Neglecting to follow up after a meaningful discussion.
  • Assuming brief interactions suffice for meaningful engagement.

 

Tangible Gifts

Tangible Gifts = the expression of appreciation or care through physical items that convey thoughtfulness, value, or recognition. These gifts are chosen to reflect the recipient’s preferences, needs, or interests, emphasizing the sentiment behind the gesture rather than the item's monetary value.

How we support this style:

  • We provide personalized gifts to celebrate employee milestones such as work anniversaries, promotions, or achievements.
  • We recognize special occasions like birthdays with thoughtful gifts tailored to each individual’s preferences.
  • We create care packages for employees during busy seasons or challenging projects, showing support through tangible gestures.
  • We distribute customized holiday gifts to demonstrate appreciation and spread goodwill.
  • We reward exceptional performance with meaningful items that reflect the individual’s contributions.
  • We offer surprise gifts during company-wide initiatives to acknowledge collective effort and foster excitement.
  • We create “welcome kits” for new hires, featuring thoughtful items that help them feel part of the team.
  • We send thank-you packages to employees after major projects, acknowledging their hard work in a tangible way.
Do

Manifestations:

  • Giving a personalized gift tailored to one’s hobbies or interests.
  • Surprising a team member with their favorite coffee or snack.
  • Celebrating milestones with thoughtful items, like flowers or treats.
  • Creating a thoughtful gift bundle for stressful times.
  • Gifting event tickets to something they are passionate about.
  • Sending holiday-themed gifts during festive seasons.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Choosing generic or impersonal gifts that lack thought.
  • Focusing on the monetary value rather than the sentiment.
  • Giving items unrelated to the recipient’s preferences or interests.
  • Overlooking cultural or personal sensitivities in gift choices.
  • Failing to acknowledge special occasions with appropriate gifts.
  • Giving items that require significant maintenance or cost to use.
  • Offering identical gifts to everyone without personalization.
  • Choosing overly extravagant gifts that might cause discomfort.

 

Physical Touch

Physical Touch = the expression of care or appreciation through appropriate and consensual physical gestures, such as handshakes, pats on the back, or hugs, to convey warmth, encouragement, or connection.

How we support this style:

  • We encourage handshakes as a professional and warm greeting to foster connection in in-person interactions.
  • We celebrate team successes with high-fives or fist bumps as symbols of camaraderie and shared achievement.
  • We provide guidelines to ensure all physical interactions align with cultural norms and individual comfort levels.
  • We encourage celebratory gestures, such as clapping or standing ovations, to add an element of shared enthusiasm.
  • We create opportunities for team-building activities that involve non-intrusive physical collaboration, such as games or sports.
  • We foster a culture of inclusivity where physical gestures are seen as one of many ways to express care and appreciation.
  • We celebrate personal milestones with optional gestures, such as hugs for those who are comfortable.
  • We educate managers on reading body language to determine when physical touch is appropriate and welcomed.
Do

Manifestations:

  • Offering a firm handshake as a greeting or farewell.
  • Giving a congratulatory pat on the back.
  • Offering a high-five to celebrate a success.
  • Providing a warm, appropriate hug when comfort is needed.
  • Resting a hand on someone’s arm briefly to show encouragement.
  • Offering a gentle tap on the arm to get someone’s attention.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Offering physical gestures without ensuring consent or comfort.
  • Ignoring personal or cultural boundaries regarding touch.
  • Using touch in situations where it feels forced or inappropriate.
  • Overusing physical gestures, making them lose their meaning.
  • Using overly casual or intimate gestures that feel unprofessional.
  • Being too forceful or awkward with gestures like handshakes.
  • Offering physical gestures that may be misunderstood or misinterpreted.
