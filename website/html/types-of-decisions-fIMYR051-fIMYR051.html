Types of Decisions

What decisions can we make?

Note: This is demo content for a fictional SaaS company.
Decision = the process of making a choice or reaching a conclusion after considering options, facts, or evidence.

 

By Scope

Who is affected by the decision?

 

Strategic Decisions

Strategic Decisions = the high-level decisions focused on setting long-term goals, overall direction, and priorities for an organization, often involving significant resource allocation and impact on the organization's future.

How we support such decisions:

  • We provide robust data analytics tools to equip individuals with actionable insights for informed decision-making.
  • We offer strategic planning frameworks to streamline the evaluation and prioritization of opportunities.
  • We ensure access to cross-functional expertise to evaluate decisions from diverse perspectives.
  • We invest in leadership training programs that enhance critical thinking and strategic foresight.
  • We use scenario planning to prepare for various outcomes and guide individuals in choosing resilient strategies.
  • We provide clear communication channels to share organizational goals and ensure alignment across teams.
  • We offer mentoring programs where experienced leaders guide individuals in making impactful strategic choices.
  • We recognize and reward individuals who make bold, successful strategic moves that drive growth.
  • We establish measurable criteria to evaluate the success of strategic decisions and refine future approaches.
Do

Needed for:

  • Establishing the organizationâ€™s long-term vision and mission.
  • Setting overarching goals and objectives for future growth.
  • Shaping company culture and overarching values.
  • Defining the scope of operations and market positioning.
  • Allocating major resources to pursue identified opportunities.
  • Addressing significant risks that threaten organization's future.
  • Responding to significant shifts in the industry landscape.
  • Deciding on mergers, acquisitions, or partnerships.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Relying on incomplete or outdated data for long-term planning.
  • Disregarding organizational culture when setting long-term goals.
  • Setting conflicting objectives that create organizational confusion.
  • Ignoring stakeholder analysis or input during major decisions.
  • Overemphasizing past successes while ignoring future challenges.
  • Failing to assess the feasibility of strategies before implementation.
  • Misjudging the potential of emerging technologies or trends.
  • Allowing personal biases to influence high-level priorities.

 

 

Tactical Decisions

Tactical Decisions = the medium-term decisions aimed at translating strategic goals into specific actions, plans, or initiatives, focusing on optimizing the allocation of resources and processes to achieve strategic objectives effectively.

How we support such decisions:

  • We provide clear guidelines that align tactical decisions with our strategic goals.
  • We offer decision-making frameworks to help individuals evaluate options effectively.
  • We ensure access to real-time data to support informed choices.
  • We encourage cross-departmental collaboration for broader perspectives.
  • We train our team members in critical thinking and problem-solving skills.
  • We prioritize resource allocation tools that enable optimization of time, budget, and personnel.
  • We use analytics to predict outcomes and guide decision-making.
  • We establish regular check-ins to monitor progress and address challenges early.
  • We reward decision-makers who demonstrate alignment with company objectives.
  • We document and share lessons learned from past decisions to improve future actions.
Do

Needed for:

  • Breaking down strategic goals into actionable plans.
  • Planning resource allocation to departments or teams.
  • Designing workflows and systems to meet medium-term goals.
  • Coordinating cross-functional collaboration to ensure alignment.
  • Determining marketing campaigns to target specific audiences.
  • Establishing performance metrics for evaluating effectiveness.
  • Selecting technologies or tools to support strategic execution.
  • Managing budgets and timelines for specific projects.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Overcommitting resources to non-essential initiatives.
  • Misaligning tactical plans with strategic objectives.
  • Misinterpreting strategic priorities due to vague communication.
  • Developing overly complex action plans that hinder execution.
  • Ignoring departmental resource limitations during planning.
  • Rushing decisions without thoroughly analyzing alternatives.
  • Failing to account for dependencies across tactical initiatives.
  • Ignoring the training or development needed for tactical success.
  • Neglecting to monitor the ongoing effectiveness of tactical plans.

 

Operational Decisions

Operational Decisions = the short-term, day-to-day decisions that deal with executing tasks, managing workflows, and addressing immediate needs, ensuring that tactical and strategic plans are implemented effectively.

How we support such decisions:

  • We ensure clear, well-documented processes to guide decision-making at every operational level.
  • We provide employees with access to real-time data and tools to make informed choices quickly.
  • We encourage a culture of accountability, empowering individuals to take ownership of their decisions.
  • We offer ongoing training to keep teams aligned with current best practices and operational standards.
  • We maintain open communication channels to allow rapid escalation of issues when necessary.
  • We set clear performance metrics to help guide daily decisions and align with broader goals.
  • We support autonomy in decision-making while providing managerial oversight when needed.
  • We reward proactive problem-solving to reinforce a culture of confident, effective operational decision-making.
Do

Needed for:

  • Scheduling daily tasks and workforce assignments.
  • Resolving immediate customer issues or complaints.
  • Handling routine financial transactions or approvals.
  • Assigning team responsibilities for ongoing tasks.
  • Addressing equipment or system malfunctions in real time.
  • Tracking and reporting daily performance metrics.
  • Optimizing efficiency in routine activities and processes.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Rushing daily decisions without considering consequences.
  • Ignoring feedback from frontline staff on operational realities.
  • Delaying decisions that require immediate action for resolution.
  • Neglecting to regularly review and update operational workflows.
  • Avoiding delegation, leading to bottlenecks in decision-making.
  • Failing to consult relevant data before making routine decisions.
  • Allowing personal preferences to override efficiency needs.

 

By Structure

How routine is the decision?

 

Structured Decisions

Structured Decisions = the type of decisions that are routine, repetitive, and well-defined, often relying on established procedures, rules, or algorithms for resolution. They typically involve clearly defined inputs and outputs, making them straightforward to automate or standardize.

How we support such decisions:

  • We establish clear procedures and guidelines to streamline decision-making.
  • We utilize advanced algorithms and software to automate repetitive tasks.
  • We provide training to ensure employees are proficient in applying standard procedures.
  • We maintain a centralized repository of rules and protocols for easy reference.
  • We implement decision-support systems to ensure consistent outcomes.
  • We use analytics to evaluate the effectiveness of standardized decision processes.
  • We offer toolkits and templates to simplify repetitive decision-making tasks.
  • We integrate structured decision frameworks into daily workflows for efficiency.
Do

Needed for:

  • Addressing routine tasks that follow a predictable pattern.
  • Handling well-defined problems with clear solutions.
  • Ensuring consistency and efficiency in decision-making processes.
  • Automating decisions with predefined algorithms or software.
  • Reducing errors and variability in standardized processes.
  • Supporting operational-level decision-making in organizations.
  • Implementing solutions in scenarios with complete data.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Overcomplicating simple, rule-based processes unnecessarily.
  • Ignoring predefined rules or procedures during execution.
  • Failing to verify data accuracy before using standard algorithms.
  • Excessively relying on automation without adequate oversight.
  • Neglecting to update decision rules after operational changes.
  • Overlooking exceptions that fall outside predefined parameters.
  • Relying on outdated systems or tools for decision-making.

 

Unstructured Decisions

Unstructured Decisions = the type of decisions that are novel, complex, and lack a clear procedure or framework, often requiring judgment, intuition, and creativity to resolve. They involve uncertain or incomplete information, making them less predictable and harder to formalize.

How we support such decisions:

  • We foster a culture of creative problem-solving by encouraging employees to think beyond conventional frameworks.
  • We provide access to a wide range of data and insights, empowering decision-makers with as much context as possible.
  • We invest in training programs focused on developing intuition and judgment for complex situations.
  • We create cross-functional teams to bring diverse perspectives and expertise to novel problems.
  • We encourage calculated risk-taking and treat failures as opportunities for learning.
  • We use advanced analytics and AI tools to fill gaps in incomplete or uncertain information.
  • We give employees the autonomy to experiment with innovative approaches and solutions.
  • We encourage scenario planning and â€œwhat-ifâ€ analyses to navigate uncertain situations effectively.
Do

Needed for:

  • Tackling complex, unique problems without predefined solutions.
  • Evaluating situations with unclear or conflicting information.
  • Adapting to dynamic environments or rapidly changing conditions.
  • Addressing strategic-level issues that impact long-term goals.
  • Developing innovative solutions for unprecedented challenges.
  • Involving collaborative input from experts or stakeholders.
  • Navigating high-risk situations with uncertain outcomes.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Underestimating the complexity of the problem at hand.
  • Assuming past experiences apply directly to new problems.
  • Failing to allocate sufficient time for thorough evaluation.
  • Ignoring the need for collaboration or diverse perspectives.
  • Over-relying on intuition without considering available evidence.
  • Failing to reassess decisions when new information is available.
  • Avoiding proactive planning, leading to reactive decision-making.
  • Allowing emotional reactions to overshadow rational judgment.

 

By Approach

How is the solution decided?

 

Maximizing Decisions

Maximizing Decisions = the type of decisions that aim to achieve the best possible outcome by thoroughly analyzing and comparing all available options to identify the most advantageous choice.

How we support such decisions:

  • We prioritize data-driven decision-making by providing access to robust analytics tools.
  • We invest in training to enhance employees' critical thinking and problem-solving skills.
  • We provide comprehensive market research to support informed evaluations.
  • We utilize advanced decision-support software to streamline complex comparisons.
  • We create frameworks and templates for systematic evaluation of alternatives.
  • We support team-based decision-making to leverage diverse perspectives.
  • We encourage experimentation and pilot testing to reduce uncertainty in major decisions.
  • We promote the use of scenario planning to anticipate potential outcomes.
Do

Needed for:

  • Ensuring the best possible outcome in high-stakes scenarios.
  • Addressing problems where precision and accuracy are critical.
  • Solving problems that require extensive analysis and forecasting.
  • Making strategic choices that involve significant investments.
  • Designing systems or solutions for efficiency or performance.
  • Pursuing opportunities where small outcome differences matter.
  • Ignoring past successful decisions for guidance or benchmarks.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Overanalyzing options, leading to decision paralysis.
  • Struggling to prioritize key factors over minor details.
  • Neglecting to set clear criteria for evaluating options.
  • Spending time gathering information with diminishing value.
  • Ignoring practical constraints like time, budget, or resources.
  • Assuming that more analysis always leads to a better decision.

 

Satisficing Decisions

Satisficing Decisions = the type of decisions that aim to meet a specific standard or requirement by selecting the first option that satisfies the criteria, without seeking the best possible choice.

How we support such decisions:

  • We prioritize speed and efficiency over exhaustive analysis when time is of the essence.
  • We provide decision-making frameworks that emphasize "good enough" solutions rather than perfection.
  • We invest in tools that offer quick access to relevant data to support satisficing decisions.
  • We train teams to focus on achieving acceptable outcomes within deadlines.
  • We recognize and reward decisions that meet objectives, even if they aren't optimal.
  • We empower employees to act autonomously within pre-defined guidelines.
  • We foster a culture where making a timely decision is valued over delaying for a potentially better one.
  • We evaluate satisficing decisions based on outcomes rather than hypothetical alternatives.
Do

Needed for:

  • Prioritizing efficiency over thoroughness in fast-paced contexts.
  • Reducing effort in situations with limited resources or energy.
  • Addressing decisions with minimal consequences or low stakes.
  • Making decisions with incomplete or uncertain information.
  • Simplifying decision-making for routine or repetitive tasks.
  • Adapting to situations where analysis costs outweigh the benefits.
  • Preventing decision fatigue when faced with too many options.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Defining vague or unclear criteria for an acceptable option.
  • Rushing the decision without considering viable alternatives.
  • Ignoring critical information due to a focus on speed or ease.
  • Neglecting long-term consequences for short-term convenience.
  • Allowing cognitive biases to overly influence the decision.
  • Skipping steps in the decision-making process, causing errors.
  • Using satisficing for critical decisions needing deeper analysis.

 

By Impact

How impactful is the decision?

 

Major Decisions

Major Decisions = the high-impact decisions that significantly influence outcomes, involve substantial consequences, and require careful consideration due to their long-term or far-reaching effects.

How we support such decisions:

  • We prioritize clarity by providing individuals with all relevant data and insights to make informed decisions.
  • We empower decision-makers with access to advanced analytics and predictive tools to assess potential outcomes.
  • We foster a culture of accountability, ensuring everyone understands the weight and impact of their choices.
  • We encourage collaboration by connecting individuals with subject matter experts and cross-functional teams.
  • We invest in continuous training to enhance decision-making skills and strategic thinking.
  • We define clear decision-making frameworks to guide individuals through complex scenarios.
  • We ensure transparency in decision-making, allowing lessons learned to be shared across the organization.
Do

Needed for:

  • Defining the overall direction or purpose of a project or initiative.
  • Setting long-term goals to guide efforts and resource allocation.
  • Tackling key challenges or opportunities with broad implications.
  • Mitigating risks that could jeopardize success or sustainability.
  • Assessing scenarios with major impact and irreversible outcomes.
  • Coordinating efforts across multiple stakeholders or departments.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Overlooking long-term consequences in favor of short-term gains.
  • Ignoring input from key stakeholders or affected parties.
  • Failing to align the decision with overarching goals or strategies.
  • Delaying decisions excessively, leading to missed opportunities.
  • Adding unnecessary layers to the decision-making process.
  • Relying solely on intuition without supporting evidence or rationale.

 

Minor Decisions

Minor Decisions = the moderate-impact decisions that address specific situations, involve limited consequences, and typically focus on outcomes with a narrower scope or localized effects.

How we support such decisions:

  • We provide clear guidelines to streamline decision-making processes for specific scenarios.
  • We empower teams with autonomy to address localized challenges effectively.
  • We encourage quick action by establishing pre-approved parameters for minor decisions.
  • We foster a culture of accountability, where individuals feel responsible for their choices.
  • We offer access to data and insights to help make informed decisions promptly.
  • We implement lightweight approval processes to maintain agility without unnecessary bottlenecks.
  • We utilize tools and platforms that simplify the evaluation of options and outcomes.
Do

Needed for:

  • Managing specific areas or functions within an ongoing process.
  • Implementing solutions for short-term issues or inefficiencies.
  • Approving adjustments or optimizations within a defined scope.
  • Balancing resources or schedules for a localized activity.
  • Delegating tasks or responsibilities to team members effectively.
  • Coordinating logistics for specific events or deliverables.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Failing to consider the broader context of the decision.
  • Making decisions too quickly without considering alternatives.
  • Underestimating a decision's impact on other processes.
  • Failing to communicate decisions effectively, causing confusion.
  • Not reviewing past outcomes to inform similar decisions.
  • Overanalyzing small decisions, delaying progress.

 

Micro Decisions

Micro Decisions = the low-impact, immediate decisions made in real-time, often routine or subconscious, that contribute incrementally to larger actions or goals.

How we support such decisions:

  • We prioritize accessible tools and resources to streamline decision-making in routine scenarios.
  • We train employees to align their micro-decisions with our overarching business goals and priorities.
  • We implement intuitive workflows that reduce cognitive load, allowing for quick, effective decisions.
  • We provide feedback loops for routine decisions, enabling continuous improvement over time.
  • We simplify processes to reduce friction and ensure that micro-decisions donâ€™t become bottlenecks.
  • We ensure clarity in roles so individuals know what decisions they are empowered to make.
  • We create a library of best practices for common scenarios to serve as reference points.
Do

Needed for:

  • Deciding on the sequence of actions within a task.
  • Adapting actions in real-time to feedback or circumstances.
  • Making incremental adjustments to improve during work.
  • Prioritizing subtasks or steps based on situational needs.
  • Recognizing and mitigating minor risks before they escalate.
  • Reacting to minor interruptions or unexpected developments.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Acting impulsively without considering the context or needs.
  • Ignoring feedback or cues that require real-time adjustments.
  • Overthinking simple choices, leading to inefficiencies.
  • Losing sight of larger objectives by focusing too much on details.
  • Relying on routine actions without considering improvements.
  • Allowing subconscious biases to influence habitual decisions.

 

By Contribution

Who is making the decision?

 

Individual Decisions

Individual Decisions = the choices made by a single person, based on their personal judgment, knowledge, and preferences, without direct input or collaboration from others.

How we support such decisions:

  • We empower individuals with access to relevant data and tools to make informed decisions.
  • We trust our team members to exercise their expertise in areas where they have the most knowledge.
  • We encourage decisive action by streamlining approval processes for low-risk decisions.
  • We provide clear frameworks and guidelines to help align individual choices with company goals.
  • We celebrate autonomy by recognizing and rewarding successful independent decisions.
  • We create a culture of accountability, where individuals take ownership of their outcomes.
  • We ensure transparency in expectations, so team members understand their decision-making boundaries.
Do

Needed for:

  • Managing tasks with limited scope or minor impact.
  • Reacting to emergencies where immediate action is required.
  • Empowering individuals with specialized expertise or authority.
  • Avoiding delays from group coordination or consensus-building.
  • Assuming responsibility in roles requiring individual authority.
  • Showing leadership by making clear and decisive choices.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Relying solely on intuition without analyzing facts or data.
  • Overestimating one's knowledge or expertise on complex issues.
  • Letting personal biases overly influence the decision.
  • Avoiding input from others that could enhance outcomes.
  • Taking responsibility for decisions meant for group accountability.
  • Misjudging the impact of individual choices on group dynamics.

 

Group Decisions

Group Decisions = the choices made collectively by a team or group of individuals, typically involving discussion, negotiation, and consensus-building to arrive at a shared conclusion.

How we support such decisions:

  • We prioritize clear frameworks to guide group decision-making, ensuring discussions stay focused and productive.
  • We provide training on effective communication and negotiation to equip individuals with the skills needed for consensus-building.
  • We emphasize the importance of defined roles, ensuring everyone understands their contributions to the decision-making process.
  • We implement structured decision-making processes, such as assigning moderators or using decision matrices, to streamline outcomes.
  • We invest in collaboration technologies that simplify sharing ideas and tracking input from all team members.
  • We celebrate and reward collective achievements, reinforcing the value of working together to reach decisions.
  • We offer clarity on goals and expectations, aligning the groupâ€™s focus toward a shared vision before discussions begin.
  • We promote accountability by assigning clear ownership for decisions and ensuring follow-through.
Do

Needed for:

  • Addressing complex problems that require collective expertise.
  • Mitigating bias by balancing input from varied viewpoints.
  • Sharing accountability for decisions with widespread implications.
  • Reducing resistance by involving those impacted in the process.
  • Planning large-scale events or initiatives requiring coordination.
  • Establishing team norms or guidelines for effective collaboration.
  • Addressing ethical dilemmas where consensus is essential.
Don't

Mistakes:

  • Focusing too much on achieving consensus, leading to groupthink.
  • Ignoring time constraints and delaying the process unnecessarily.
  • Allowing unclear roles or responsibilities to confuse the process.
  • Letting interpersonal conflicts derail productive discussions.
  • Misjudging groupâ€™s capacity to handle certain complex decisions.
  • Ignoring individual expertise in favor of collective agreement.
  • Allowing dominant voices to suppress differing opinions.
