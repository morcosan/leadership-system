Types of Trust

What do we trust?

Interpersonal

Do we trust our peers?

 

Competence Trust

Competence Trust = the confidence we have in another's abilities, skills, and knowledge to effectively perform tasks or fulfill responsibilities. It’s built on demonstrated expertise, reliability, and consistency in delivering expected outcomes.

How we nurture trust:

  • We prioritize hiring individuals who demonstrate both skill and passion for their work.
  • We provide clear goals so every team member understands how their contributions impact the company.
  • We recognize and reward expertise, fostering confidence in individual abilities.
  • We encourage continuous learning, offering resources for skill development and personal growth.
  • We trust individuals to make decisions in their areas of expertise, reducing micromanagement.
  • We celebrate individual achievements to reinforce trust in their competence.
  • We create platforms for individuals to showcase their skills and share insights.
  • We emphasize accountability, ensuring each person owns their responsibilities.
  • We provide constructive feedback, helping individuals refine their skills without undermining their confidence.
  • We trust individuals to mentor others, spreading their expertise across the organization.
Do

When trust is present:

  • Team members confidently delegate tasks, knowing their colleagues have the skills to deliver quality results.
  • Organizations reduce redundant oversight and review processes, improving operational efficiency.
  • Knowledge sharing increases naturally as people trust their colleagues' expertise and judgment.
  • Stress levels decrease since people aren't constantly second-guessing others' capabilities.
Don't

When trust is missing:

  • Excessive micromanagement wastes time and creates frustration at all levels.
  • Decision-making slows significantly as every choice requires multiple approvals and validations.
  • Innovation stalls because people fear others won't execute their ideas properly.
  • Team morale suffers as people feel their competence is constantly questioned.

 

Emotional Trust

Emotional Trust = the confidence we have in another's intentions, character, and emotional reliability. It’s built on the belief that someone genuinely cares about our well-being, values the relationship, and will act with integrity and empathy.

How we nurture trust:

  • We create a safe environment where individuals feel valued and respected.
  • We encourage open and honest communication without fear of judgment or reprisal.
  • We actively listen to individual concerns and act on feedback to build mutual respect.
  • We support individuals during personal challenges, demonstrating care beyond work.
  • We respect boundaries and honor personal commitments outside the workplace.
  • We celebrate individual diversity, fostering inclusivity and understanding.
  • We encourage vulnerability by leading with empathy and authenticity.
  • We maintain confidentiality to ensure individuals feel secure in sharing sensitive matters.
  • We acknowledge emotions and validate experiences, creating deeper personal connections.
  • We foster team camaraderie through shared experiences that build trust and emotional bonds.
Do

When trust is present:

  • People feel safe to share ideas, concerns, and feedback without fear of judgment.
  • Teams bounce back more quickly from challenges because they support and rely on one another emotionally.
  • Employees are more committed and enthusiastic about their work when they feel emotionally secure.
  • Disputes are resolved amicably, as emotional trust ensures good intentions and reduces defensiveness.
Don't

When trust is missing:

  • Team members withhold thoughts and ideas, leading to misunderstandings and inefficiency.
  • Employees feel unsupported and undervalued, leading to dissatisfaction and disengagement.
  • A lack of emotional safety creates a tense work environment, leading to burnout and reduced productivity.
  • Constructive criticism is often misinterpreted as personal attacks, hampering improvement and development.

 

Integrity Trust

Integrity Trust = the confidence we have in another's moral character, ethical principles, and commitment to doing what is right. It’s grounded in the belief that someone will act honestly, transparently, and in alignment with shared values, even when faced with difficult choices.

How we nurture trust:

  • We uphold transparency in our actions, ensuring individuals trust our words and decisions.
  • We consistently follow through on promises, demonstrating reliability.
  • We encourage honesty and authenticity in all interactions, building a culture of integrity.
  • We model ethical behavior at all levels, inspiring individuals to do the same.
  • We hold individuals accountable for their actions, reinforcing trust through fairness.
  • We reward truth-telling, even when it’s uncomfortable or inconvenient.
  • We maintain clear policies to ensure everyone operates with a shared understanding of integrity.
  • We address mistakes openly and responsibly, showing a commitment to doing the right thing.
  • We discourage favoritism and promote equal treatment, fostering fairness.
  • We encourage individuals to voice concerns about unethical behavior without fear of retaliation.
Do

When trust is present:

  • Decisions are guided by shared values and a belief in others' honesty, reducing ambiguity and conflict.
  • People take responsibility for their actions, knowing integrity is valued and expected.
  • Disputes are minimized when all parties act in good faith and adhere to ethical standards.
  • Workers are more likely to stay in organizations where integrity is upheld, fostering long-term commitment.
Don't

When trust is missing:

  • Mistrust in integrity leads to suspicion, accusations, and frequent disagreements.
  • Decisions become flawed when stakeholders suspect hidden agendas or dishonest intent.
  • Leaders and team members lose authority and influence when their integrity is questioned.
  • Talented employees leave organizations perceived as unethical or unreliable.

 

 

Organizational

Do we trust our organization?

 

Leadership Trust

Leadership Trust = the confidence we place in a leader's ability to guide, inspire, and make decisions in the best interest of the team or organization. It stems from a leader's consistent demonstration of competence, fairness, transparency, accountability, and empathy.

How we nurture trust:

  • We ensure leaders and managers communicate a clear vision that inspires confidence and alignment.
  • We expect leaders to lead by example, demonstrating consistency between words and actions.
  • We prioritize transparency in decision-making to build organizational trust.
  • We encourage leaders to actively listen and respond to team feedback.
  • We empower managers to make informed decisions, showcasing their expertise and judgment.
  • We emphasize ethical leadership, reinforcing trust through fairness and integrity.
  • We provide leadership training to ensure managers excel in guiding and supporting their teams.
  • We celebrate leaders who prioritize team success over personal gain.
  • We foster accountability at all levels of leadership, ensuring trust through reliability and ownership.
  • We evaluate leaders on their ability to build and maintain trust within their teams.
Do

When trust is present:

  • Trust in leadership inspires employees to align with organizational goals and invest emotionally in their work.
  • Open and transparent communication flourishes when employees trust their leaders to be honest and approachable.
  • Trustworthy leaders inspire confidence during transitions, ensuring smoother adaptation to new initiatives or challenges.
  • Employees are more likely to stay in an organization where they trust their leaders, reducing recruitment costs.
Don't

When trust is missing:

  • Employees resist new policies and initiatives, stalling organizational progress.
  • Leaders struggle to maintain authority as skepticism erodes respect.
  • Gossip and rumors proliferate, exacerbating workplace tension and misinformation.
  • Performance reviews and feedback sessions are viewed with suspicion, reducing their effectiveness.

 

Systemic Trust

Systemic Trust = the confidence we have in the reliability, fairness, and integrity of structures, systems, and institutions to function effectively and equitably. It’s grounded in the belief that processes, rules, and norms are designed and implemented to uphold justice, transparency, and consistency, benefiting all stakeholders.

How we nurture trust:

  • We design processes that are transparent, ensuring clarity and fairness across the organization.
  • We implement systems that prioritize reliability and minimize disruptions.
  • We regularly review and improve processes to maintain alignment with organizational goals.
  • We ensure policies are consistent and applied equitably to build trust in the structure.
  • We provide clear documentation and guidelines to eliminate ambiguity in procedures.
  • We prioritize data security and privacy, fostering trust in our systems.
  • We establish feedback loops to address gaps and ensure continuous improvement in processes.
  • We use technology to streamline workflows, ensuring efficiency and dependability.
  • We train teams on systems and processes to enhance trust through competence and familiarity.
  • We create escalation pathways to resolve issues quickly and transparently within the structure.
Do

When trust is present:

  • Systems promote accountability without unnecessary micromanagement, empowering individuals.
  • Resources are allocated effectively, with trust ensuring there is no favoritism or waste.
  • Workflows are streamlined, as trust eliminates the need for excessive oversight or redundant approvals.
  • Employees focus on innovation and value-creation rather than navigating bureaucratic hurdles.
Don't

When trust is missing:

  • Employees focus on personal gain, doubting the system’s ability to ensure fairness for all.
  • Organizational resources are wasted on enforcing policies rather than promoting growth.
  • Decision-making becomes reactive rather than strategic, as uncertainty hampers long-term planning.
  • Teams hesitate to align their efforts with organizational goals, fearing system-level inequities.

 

Cultural Trust

Cultural Trust = the confidence in shared norms, values, and practices within a community or society to foster mutual understanding, respect, and cooperation. It is rooted in the belief that cultural frameworks will promote inclusivity, empathy, and fairness, enabling diverse individuals and groups to interact harmoniously.

How we nurture trust:

  • We foster a culture of respect where every employee feels valued and heard.
  • We celebrate diversity, embracing varied perspectives and experiences.
  • We promote collaboration over competition, building trust through teamwork.
  • We uphold company values consistently, demonstrating alignment between words and actions.
  • We encourage openness and transparency at all levels, nurturing trust in our intentions.
  • We recognize and reward behaviors that strengthen the culture of trust.
  • We prioritize fairness in opportunities, ensuring equal treatment across the organization.
  • We celebrate successes together, creating a shared sense of achievement.
  • We invest in initiatives that enhance employee well-being and satisfaction.
  • We communicate openly about challenges and involve employees in solutions, reinforcing trust in the company.
Do

When trust is present:

  • Employees feel safe expressing their opinions and ideas, leading to a richer exchange of perspectives.
  • Communication across different cultural and organizational backgrounds becomes more effective.
  • A sense of belonging emerges, reducing turnover and increasing employee loyalty.
  • Conflict resolution becomes easier, with cultural trust encouraging empathy and understanding.
Don't

When trust is missing:

  • Misunderstandings and stereotypes flourish, leading to conflicts and alienation.
  • Collaboration is hindered as team members struggle to bridge cultural differences.
  • Employees feel marginalized or excluded, reducing morale and engagement.
  • High-performing talent may leave due to perceived cultural insensitivity or exclusion.

 

 

Governmental

Do we trust our public institutions?

 

Governmental Trust

Governmental Trust = the confidence citizens have in their government to act responsibly, transparently, and in the best interest of the public. It is rooted in the belief that government institutions, officials, and policies are ethical, effective, and accountable, adhering to principles of justice, equity, and the rule of law.

How we nurture trust:

  • We align our policies with regulatory standards, ensuring full compliance and ethical governance.
  • We actively engage with government bodies to build constructive relationships.
  • We advocate for policies that promote innovation and fair competition in our industry.
  • We maintain transparency in our lobbying efforts, ensuring trust in our intentions and actions.
  • We collaborate with policymakers to address industry challenges and create win-win solutions.
  • We communicate openly with employees about our stance on governmental matters to build internal trust.
  • We support initiatives that benefit society while aligning with our business goals.
  • We invest in understanding changing regulations to adapt seamlessly and remain trustworthy.
  • We uphold ethical lobbying practices to influence policies responsibly and transparently.
  • We work to demonstrate that our actions contribute positively to both the industry and the wider community.
Do

When trust is present:

  • Trust in government enables confidence in public services, such as healthcare and education, benefiting employees and their families.
  • Employees trust that corporate practices comply with ethical standards enforced by the government.
  • Worker rights and protections are upheld reliably, fostering a sense of safety and fairness.
  • Workers have faith in government-led initiatives, such as upskilling programs or grants, to enhance career growth opportunities.
Don't

When trust is missing:

  • Employees fear job insecurity as unstable government policies affect economic conditions.
  • Employees lose confidence in social services, leading to concerns about healthcare, education, and retirement.
  • Distrust in government casts doubt on the company’s compliance with ethical and legal standards.
  • Political instability causes stress and uncertainty about the future of the company and the economy.
